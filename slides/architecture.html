Preprocessing — two channels per driver

The layer that changed. Each analyst receives these two channels about its own driver and nothing else.

Engineered features — the "what"

Deterministic measurements computed from the raw series, like the metrics an economist chooses to put on their dashboard.

  • levels, changes, YoY / QoQ
  • moving averages, spreads
  • distance from a reference (target, neutral)
No score · no direction · no signal

Driver-specific text — the "why"

The context behind those measurements: why the driver moved and what the people who move it are saying about it.

  • passages bearing on this driver only
  • change vs the previous document
  • partitioned, never the shared full document
Preprocessing rule — open, team assessing

  1. PARTITION THE DATA. A shared document collapses analyst independence: feeding every analyst the same FOMC statement moved average pairwise correlation from 0.221 to 0.339 and drove faithfulness negative for inflation (−0.171) and term premium (−0.050) — those analysts tracked other drivers more than their own. Both channels are partitioned for this reason.
  2. FEED EVIDENCE, NEVER THE ANSWER. The superseded design put the finished DriverView — direction and conviction included — inside the prompt and asked the model to refine it. Agreement with the formula ran own_corr 0.965; "the LLM added nothing" was the prompt's doing. A feature named like inflation_momentum_signal would reintroduce exactly this.
  3. GAUGE THE LEAK. Richer inputs make date-blinding harder, not easier — a CPI YoY print identifies its quarter with or without a date stamp. Date-blind prompting plus a post-cutoff control window are both required; the 2019–2024 window sits entirely pre-cutoff, so the current leak test is inconclusive by construction.
  4. LLMs REASON, FORMULAS SIZE. Analysts and PMs are both LLMs, so view convergence can now occur at either layer and needs a gauge at both. Sizing, netting, and risk stay deterministic and re-clip everything above them.
